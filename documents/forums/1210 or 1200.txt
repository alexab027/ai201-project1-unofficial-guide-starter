COMMENT:
Terrible idea and terrible logic.

PNR is there, and has always been there, so new freshman can acclimate to the MIT pace. Not trying to scare you, your AIME skills are nothing compared to what you’re about to experience.

This whole thinking of loading up on hard classes freshman year is half-baked. The point of college is to learn the material in a coherent way. You’re not trying to take all your junior and senior classes and do them freshman year so you can screw them up and ink out a barely passing grade.

I took the old version of 1200, loved the class, and cannot imagine taking 1210 without it. I even took a class like 1200 in high school and 1200 was like 8 times as hard.

PNR is there as a safety net so you can rewire your discipline and study habits for a new normal. Don’t play these dumb “hard class scheduling” games. Take all classes and in the order they should be taken. It’ll be hard enough when you do that, so don’t make it harder on yourself.

Welcome and best of luck!

COMMENT:
I know registration has already taken place, but as an undergraduate associate advisor the main thing I'd worry about is lack of previous proof experience. You should have all the necessary background for any course you decide to try, even if you're genuinely interested in it. That being said, you might want to review what topics are covered in both courses; if I recall correctly it's mainly algorithmic complexity and proofs that carry over between the two classes. If you're having any trouble or further concerns, feel free to email the professor(s) and your advisor/associate advisor.
